SDG - 13 & Indian Traditions

**Reduce Water Usage**

:

Embrace the Indian tradition of conserving water by using it judiciously. Fix any leaks, turn off taps when not in use, and practise rainwater harvesting if possible.

**Plant Trees**

Hindu traditions have long revered trees. Planting trees or participating in tree-planting drives can help combat climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide.

**Avoid Single-Use Plastics*

:

Indian traditions often emphasise the importance of respecting and protecting the environment. Avoid single-use plastics like plastic bags and utensils to reduce pollution.

**Bicycle or Walk**

:

Emulate the traditional practice of walking or cycling for short distances instead of relying on motorised transport. This reduces carbon emissions.

**Conserve Energy**

:

Implement energy-saving habits at home, such as turning off lights and appliances when not in use. Use energy-efficient bulbs and appliances to reduce electricity consumption.

**Learn About Indigenous Knowledge**

:

Explore traditional Indian knowledge related to sustainable agriculture and natural resource management. This can inspire eco-friendly practices.

**Participate in Clean-up Drives**

:

Join local clean-up initiatives or organise one in your community to clean rivers, beaches, or public spaces. This helps preserve natural environments.

**Raise Awareness**

:

Use social media and community gatherings to raise awareness about climate change and its impacts. Share information and encourage others to take action.
