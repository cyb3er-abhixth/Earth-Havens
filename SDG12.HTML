SDG - 12 & Indian Traditions

**Reduce Food Waste**

:

In Indian traditions, there is an emphasis on not wasting food. Practice portion control and try to finish the food on your plate to minimise food waste.

**Use Traditional Cloth Bags**

:

Instead of using plastic bags, adopt the tradition of using cloth bags for shopping. This reduces plastic consumption and waste.

**Recycle and Upcycle**

:

Embrace the concept of "Jugaad," which involves creative and sustainable solutions. Learn to recycle materials and upcycle old items into something useful.

**Conservation of Resources**

:

Indian traditions often emphasise the importance of conserving resources like water and electricity. Be mindful of your resource usage at home and school..

**Buy Local and Seasonal**

:

Follow the tradition of buying local and seasonal produce. This reduces the carbon footprint associated with transportation and supports local farmers.

**Repurpose Clothing**

:

Instead of constantly buying new clothes, repurpose and restyle your clothing items. You can follow traditional Indian textile arts like embroidery or tie-dye to refresh your wardrobe.

**Handmade Gifts**

:

Make handmade gifts for your friends and family instead of buying mass-produced items. This supports traditional craftsmanship and reduces the demand for factory-made products.

**Mindful Consumption**

:

Practise mindfulness in your shopping habits. Before making a purchase, ask yourself if you really need the item and if it aligns with your values of responsible consumption.
